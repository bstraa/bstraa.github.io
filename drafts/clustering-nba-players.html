Clustering NBA Players

Posted on Wed 13 April 2016 in articles

Background

Below is a screenshot of the d3.js web app I made. The real version is here.

The NBA has been using SportVU Cameras to record player data at 25x per second, resulting in an explosion of data in an already statistically dense sport. In particular, new statistics such as speed and distance have emerged.

I really enjoy strategic thinking and decision making and thought these statistics would unleash new insights into player and team analytics. Specifically, I was interested in which roles players actually fulfill. We traditionally rely on the somewhat arbitary label 'position' to describe how players fit within a team, but it is an extremely poor definition of the players actual role.

This new explosion of NBA data and this inspired me to take a closer look at player types. How could this new data help us more accurately describe the roles of players within their team? What are the actual five positions within a team?

Acquiring Data

Although there are many great resources for acquiring NBA data, I could only find one that included a rich amount of SportVu data, which I was really interested in. I was specifically interested in the new data because it would provide new insights that were missed in the past. I didn't want to just use traditional NBA statistics, I wanted to amplify them with data only SportVu cameras were picking up --- like speeds and distances.

I scraped publically available data from the NBAs Website, which spanned three seasons. There were 1200+ unique data points (unique players in unique seasons) and 800+ features (many with new data from SportVu cameras).

Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA is notoriously difficult in high dimensional feature spaces. Naturally, examining 800+ different features and their combinations would be a never-ending task. I wanted to reduce the dimensionality of the feature space before doing some poking around so I used Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to identify directions of large variation in the dataset.
